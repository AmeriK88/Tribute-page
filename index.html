Alex Honnold

Alex Honnold was born in Sacramento, California, to community college professors Dierdre Wolownick (b. 1953) and Charles Honnold (1949–2004). His father is of German ancestry and his mother is of Polish ancestry 1. Alex graduated from Mira Loma High School in Sacramento 1. He started climbing when he was 11 years old and at the age of 18, he dropped out of the University of California, Berkeley, where he was studying engineering, to focus on climbing full-time 1. Between climbs, he runs or hikes to stay in shape 1. He lives in a van, spending less than a thousand dollars a month, which allows him to climb all the time 1.


Big wall climbing

2007, Freerider (VI 5.13a, 37 pitches), Yosemite – One day free ascent with Brian Kimball

2007, Astroman (5.11c, 10 pitches) and The Rostrum (5.11c, 8 pitches), Yosemite – First-ever repeat of Peter Croft's 1987 free solo in one day

2008, Moonlight Buttress (V 5.12d, 1200 ft), Zion, Utah – First Free solo

2008, Regular Northwest Face of Half Dome, Yosemite – First free solo

2012, The Nose (VI 5.8 A2), Yosemite, El Capitan – Former speed record of 2:23:46 with Hans Florine

2012, The Regular Northwest Face of Half Dome, Yosemite – Speed solo in 1:22[

2012, Yosemite Triple Crown – Mt. Watkins, El Capitan, and Half Dome, Yosemite – Solo in 18:50

2014, Muir Wall – Shaft Variation (V 5.13b/c) – Speed record of 12 hours

2016, Complete Scream (E8 6b), Northern Ireland, United Kingdom – Free solo

2019, Arrested Development 9a (5.14d) Mount Charleston, Nevada, second ascent of sport climbing route after Jonathan Siegrist
